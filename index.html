Brand Positioning

What we're selling, to whom, and against what.

One-sentence positioning

"A room where different minds meet the same question — each from their own stance. The fun is in the friction."

Source: Travis's exec brief
What it is

You come in with a topic. You leave with a new angle. The core isn't Q&A — it's the collision of stances. The user puts a topic out; the system seats 2–3 personas suited to it. Within three minutes, they handle it from their own angles — stake a position, respond, disagree. The user can interrupt, follow up, or pivot at any time.

The gap it fills

Not the news itself — the interpretation layer. "I know what happened, but I don't know what to make of it." The gap that reporting leaves behind, picked up by people at the table. Not information. Not answers. A new angle.

What it isn't

Not an AI assistant — one voice, one answer. Not a podcast — pre-recorded, the same for every listener. Not AI companionship — flattery, not friction. Roundtable is not an assistant. Not a search engine. A room where different minds meet the same question.

The experience

Three minutes per round. Voice-first — tone is part of the stance. Non-replayable, but the card travels: topic, personas, key stances — designed to leave the product. The user leaves with an angle they didn't have before.

Lead ICP — the one we sell to

Health-content creators and knowledge workers who need interpretive depth, not just information. Open Beta leads with Health — body, mind, lifestyle — anchored by TVBS 健康2.0. Team size 2–10. Producers, editors, medical reviewers who need multiple expert angles on the same topic.

Core pain we solve

One AI voice converges on one answer. Users with complex topics — health, finance, career decisions — need perspectives that collide, not consensus. The gap isn't information. It's interpretation. Single-voice AI closes down a question. Roundtable opens it up.

The idea
One question becomes a multi-agent debate.

People do not just need one fast answer. For important questions, they need a multi-level response: different expert agents with specific perspectives looking at the same question.

One agent may focus on risk. Another on daily habits. Another on emotional context. Another on practical next steps. When those perspectives debate each other, the answer becomes more robust, more entertaining, and easier to trust than a single chatbot reply.

Three Pillars
Story

AI is no longer a search box. It is a room you can enter. The whole worldview is built around one idea: conversations are more powerful when multiple minds collide.

Signal

The roundtable is the symbol. Not a chatbot window. Not a feed. A table with empty chairs, expert voices, and a place for you to interrupt.

System

Every topic becomes a living discussion. Health, fashion, food, finance, art — each one can be previewed like a show. The product sells the feeling of joining a conversation before it explains the technology.

What they really did.
  • They built a room before they built a tool.
  • They built voices before they built answers.
  • They built participation before they built search.
