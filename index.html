Roundtable AI rollout map · plain-English view

Don’t ask one AI. Ask the whole roundtable.

A linear rollout for testing whether Roundtable can become a product people understand, share, and return to — with clear gates before bigger launch moves.

Core product idea

People do not just need one fast answer. They need a multi-level response: several expert agents with specific perspectives debating the same question, then giving a useful next step.

Strongest first topic

Health, because TVBS / 健康2.0 is a real partner channel and health questions naturally need multiple expert perspectives.

Health firstFoodTechSportFinance

Rollout timeline

Dates are less important than sequence. Each step earns the right to move to the next one.

1. Build the Health proof

Health first

Use TVBS / 健康2.0 as the first real partner channel. Show a health question becoming a multi-agent expert debate: family care, nutrition, sleep, rehab, and medical caution.

Main thing to provePeople understand Roundtable fast.
Product momentOne question becomes a lively expert debate.
Must measureFirst useful Roundtable completed.

Gate 1: Is the first experience working?

Go / slow down

Before wider launch, check if the basic loop is real: users complete a useful Roundtable, share tracking is ready, and people can explain the product after seeing it.

Pass ifUsers finish a first useful Roundtable.
Pass ifSharing clicks and shared-link opens are recorded.
If weakFix the product or explanation before sending more traffic.

2. Open beta, not full launch

Learn in public

Let more people try it, but do not pretend the product is fully proven. The public story stays simple: Generative Debate around health, with secondary examples in food, tech, sport, and finance.

Health

Main story. Partner proof through TVBS / 健康2.0.

Food

Cuisine taste debates, restaurant choice, nutrition angles.

Tech

Tool comparisons, build decisions, operator debates.

Sport

Tennis expert, coach, injury doctor, stats analyst.

Finance

Saving, spending, risk education. Not financial advice.

3. Watch the social loop

Sharing matters

Roundtable should not only be used; it should be shared. A shared link should open the same topic and expert setup so the next person can start their own session.

Share rateDo users send links or recaps?
Shared link conversionDo receivers start a session?
Time in sessionDo users stay with the debate?

Gate 2: Is this product pulling people back?

Full launch decision

Only move to a bigger launch if users share it, receivers open links, people return, and the Health experience feels real — not just a cool demo.

Pass ifRepeat use is real.
Pass ifSharing creates new sessions.
If mixedStay in beta and fix the weak part.

4. Choose the second topic from data

Not opinion

Food, tech, sport, and finance all get small tests. The next topic should be whichever one wins on sharing, time in session, and repeat use.

Food wins ifPeople share taste debates.
Sport wins ifFans stay for expert debate.
Finance wins ifPeople return for cautious education.

Gate 3: Can this become a paid partner test?

Partner test

A paid partner test means a short 30- or 60-day test with a partner, like TVBS using Roundtable inside Health 2.0 with real users. Both sides agree what success means before it starts.

Pass ifA partner sees real user value.
Pass ifThe team can support the test.
If notReturn to product and sharing loop.

Gate 4: Is it ready to scale?

Scale or hold

Only expand when the product has repeat use, a working share loop, and partner proof. If the core loop is weak, adding more topics or markets only hides the problem.

Scale ifUsers come back and share.
Scale ifPartner tests can become real rollouts.
Hold ifPeople try once but do not return.

Gate logic

At each gate, mixed signal means slow down and fix. Do not force a full launch because the calendar says so.

Gate 1

Can people finish their first useful Roundtable and understand what it is?

Gate 2

Do they share, do receivers start sessions, and do users come back?

Gate 3

Can a partner run a paid test with real users and clear success rules?

Gate 4

Is the loop strong enough to expand topics, partners, or markets?
