Don’t ask one AI. Ask the whole roundtable.

A rollout map for proving whether Generative Debate can become a product people understand, share, and return to.

Health firstFoodTechSportFinance

The idea

One question becomes a multi-agent debate.

People do not just need one fast answer. For important questions, they need a multi-level response: different expert agents with specific perspectives looking at the same question.

One agent may focus on risk. Another on daily habits. Another on emotional context. Another on practical next steps. When those perspectives debate each other, the answer becomes more robust, more entertaining, and easier to trust than a single chatbot reply.

Rollout timeline

Earn the right to keep going.

Start

Build the Health proof

Show a health question becoming a Roundtable of expert agents, then a useful recap.

Gate 1 first experience check

Is the first experience working?

Users finish a first useful Roundtable. Sharing measurement is ready: share clicks, shared-link opens, and new sessions from shared links are recorded.

Open beta

Learn in public, but do not call it fully launched

Health stays the main story. Food, tech, sport, and finance appear as secondary examples and tests.

Social loop

Watch whether people share it

A shared link should open the same topic and expert setup so the next person can start their own session.

Gate 2 launch decision

Is this product pulling people back?

Only move bigger if people share it, receivers start sessions, users spend time in the debate, and people return without being pushed.

Next topic

Pick the second topic from data

Food, tech, sport, and finance all get small tests. Choose the next topic by share rate, time in session, and repeat use — not opinion.

Gate 3 partner test check

Can this become a paid partner test?

A partner such as TVBS tries Roundtable inside their app with real users for 30 or 60 days. Both sides agree what success means before it starts.

Gate 4 scale decision

Is it ready to scale?

Only expand topics, partners, or markets when repeat use, sharing, and partner proof are real.
